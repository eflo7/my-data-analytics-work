# NFL Draft Attendance Analysis (2014 to 2025)

## Overview
This project analyzes which NFL prospects attend the NFL Draft from 2014 to 2025. The goal is to identify patterns behind attendance decisions and understand what factors influence whether a player chooses to attend draft night.

The dataset combines draft results with attendance status and additional context to support deeper analysis.

## Dataset
File: nfl_draft_attendance_2014_2025.xlsx

The dataset includes:
- Year
- Pick number
- Player name
- Position
- School
- Attendance status
- Contextual notes

## Key Questions
- Are early draft picks more likely to attend the draft?
- Do certain positions attend more frequently than others?
- Has attendance behavior changed over time?
- Are high profile prospects more likely to attend?

## Key Insights
- Players selected in the top 10 are far more likely to attend than later picks
- Quarterbacks and wide receivers show higher attendance rates compared to linemen
- Attendance drops significantly after the first round
- There is moderate variation year to year, but overall patterns remain consistent

## Why This Matters
Understanding draft attendance trends is useful for teams, agencies, and media. It helps with planning draft coverage, preparing clients for draft night decisions, and improving fan engagement.

This project connects directly to sports analytics, player branding, and media strategy.
